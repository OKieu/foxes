About Foxes

Foxes are small to medium-sized, omnivorous mammals belonging to the family Canidae. They are known for their cunning nature and adaptability to various environments. They're latin names is the Vulpes Vulpes.

Habitat

Foxes are found on every continent except Antarctica. They thrive in diverse habitats, including forests, grasslands, mountains, and deserts.

Diet

Foxes are omnivorous and have a varied diet that includes fruits, vegetables, small mammals, birds, and insects.

Fun Facts

  • Foxes are known for their playful behavior and intelligence.
  • They have excellent hearing and can hear low-frequency sounds.
  • The red fox is the most common and widely distributed species of fox.

Other Types of Foxes

There are other types of foxes not limited to our red friend, Vulpes Vulpes. The Artic Fox lives within colder climates, and can be distinctly recognized by their white fur. Their latin name is the Vulpex Lagopus.
